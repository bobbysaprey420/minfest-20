Opti-Mine_it

Every industry requires the optimization of tasks and mining industry is no different. In mining we need to set the parameters (no. of machines, no. of shifts etc.) such that the profits get maximized and time comsumed is minimum, and to do this one should have good knowledge on how to optimize the tasks given to us with constraints. Competitive programming lets us understand the different ways we can develop an optimal solution of the tasks at hand with constraints, programming the methods is necessary as it lets us avoid the same redundant work (by using libraries) and let us focus on the crux of the problem.

PAPER PRESENTATION

About

In today's dynamic world the need of the hour is to have a sustainable, economic and efficient plan to accompalish any task. Minfest20 invites students/scholars to present their papers and contribute to fostering the Industry-Academia Collaboration.

Eligibility

The participant must be pursuing an UG/PG/Diploma program in Mining or allied (Petroleum, Geology) from a recognized institute; and if you are from any other engineering branch, then you must have done or should have been doing any project which is directly related to Mining engineering. The participant may be asked to prove his/her eligibility.

Event Structure

The event consists of two rounds. First is online submission and the second is to be conducted at IIT (BHU) Varanasi during Minfest20. First round is Online submission of abstract in pdf format to be emailed to minfest@itbhu.ac.in and based on which teams will be short listed for the next round. Second round is a presentation cum questionnaire round where participants need to present their work before Judges and other teams.

Rules

  1. Individuals must register online to participate.
  2. Each team can contain a maximum of 4 participants
  3. For the preliminary round each team needs to prepare an abstract of their innovative idea.
  4. The abstract must contain the team name and the names of each member, and it should be mailed to minfest@itbhu.ac.in
  5. Project or paper must be related to Mining.
  6. Abstract shall not contain more than 200 words.
  7. Deadline for submission of abstract is 21.03.2019. Abstract will not be accepted after the deadline.
  8. Teams will be short listed on the basis of the merit of their submission. So try to cover all the important points of your work in the abstract.
  9. Short listed participants will be eligible to present their idea at IIT (BHU) Varanasi during MINFEST'20.
  10. Teams short listed for the second round must come with a proper presentation along with at least 2 hardcopies of their presentation.
  11. Each team will be provided a time limit of 10 minutes to present their ideas.
  12. The decision of the judges will be final and binding.
  13. Top three participants will get a certificate of merit and others will get a certificate of participation.

Geocarter

Geology is the study of the structure, evolution and dynamics of the Earth and its natural mineral and energy resources. Geology investigates the processes that have shaped the Earth through its 4500 million year history and uses the rock record to unravel that history. It is concerned with the real world beyond the laboratory and has direct relevance to the needs of society .Because of the ever-increasing demand for resources, the growing exposure to natural hazards, and the changing climate, geology is of considerable societal relevance. Geocarter event will check your grip on geology with your presence of mind and real life problem solving techniques. The event contains interesting geological engineering problems with geological mapping and identification of different rock and minerals.

Eligibility

The participant must be pursuing an UG/PG/Diploma program in Mining or allied courses (Petroleum, Geology) from a recognized institute. If the participant is from any other engineering branch, then he/she must have done/undergoing any project directly related to GEOLOGY. The participant may be asked to prove his/her eligibility.

Event Structure

  • Event consists of 2 elimination rounds.

Along with finding the correct specimen within time-limit, participants will have to brief about its features, properties and applications.
  • Based on the performance of round 2, participants will get points and decide their ranking. If judges get hint of any malpractices respective team will get disqualified.
